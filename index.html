Tribute to Nelson Mandela

"Do not judge me by my successes, judge me by how many times I fell down and got back up again." 

~Nelson Mandela

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, politician, and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election. Mandela was also a philanthropist who founded the Nelson Mandela Foundation, which focuses on combating HIV/AIDS and supporting rural development in South Africa.Mandela spent 27 years in prison for his anti-apartheid activism before being released in 1990.

Early Life

  • Mandela was given the name Rolihlahla, which means "troublemaker" in the Xhosa language. He was the youngest of four children.
  • Mandela was nine years old, his father died, and he was sent to live with the chief of the Thembu people, where he was groomed to become a counselor to the monarch.
  • Mandela received his primary education at a local missionary school, where he was given the name "Nelson" by a teacher.
  • In 1939, Mandela enrolled at the University of Fort Hare, the only university for black South Africans at the time, where he studied law.
  • However, Mandela was expelled from the university for participating in a student protest against the university's policies.
  • In 1941, Mandela moved to Johannesburg, where he began working as a night watchman and completed his law degree through the University of South Africa.

    • Contributions

  • Anti-Apartheid Activism: Nelson Mandela was a well-known anti-apartheid activist who devoted his life to eradicating racial inequality and segregation in South Africa. He was a key figure in the African National Congress (ANC), an organisation that used boycotts, civil disobedience, and nonviolent protests to fight apartheid.

  • Imprisonment: Mandela served a total of 27 years in prison for his anti-apartheid work, the majority of which were spent on Robben Island. Mandela stayed dedicated to his cause despite the unfavourable circumstances and cruel treatment, and his detention further strengthened his status as a remarkable figure of resiliency and resolve.

  • Negotiations and Reconciliation: Negotiations and Reconciliation: Following his release from jail in 1990, Nelson Mandela was a crucial player in efforts to peacefully abolish apartheid and usher in a democratic transition in South Africa. He promoted forgiveness and reconciliation as a way to mend the scars of the past and strove to create bridges across various racial and ethnic groupings.

  • Presidency: In a wholly representative democratic election held in 1994, Nelson Mandela was chosen as South Africa's first black president. He led the nation through a challenging time of transition and consolidation while serving as president from 1977 to 1999.
